Bicol Express Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp. Oil.
  • 5 cloves. Garlic.
  • 1 pc. Ginger.
  • 1 pc. Medium size Onion.
  • 250 g. Pork Belly.
  • 2 tbsp. Bagoong Alamang.
  • 2 cups Coconut Milk
  • 250 g. Green finger chili
  • 100 g. red chili



Instructions

  1. Sauté garlic, ginger, onion, pork and bagoong for 5 minutes.
  2. Pour coconut milk and simmer for 20-25 minutes until pork is tender.
  3. Add finger and red chili and cook for another 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Transfer into a serving plate and serve, and enjoy!

Other Popular Bicol Cuisines

  • Laing

    - Laing is a traditional Bicolano dish made from dried taro leaves simmered in coconut milk (gata) with chili, garlic, onions, and often includes meat or dried fish. It is known for its rich, creamy texture and spicy flavor, reflecting the Bicol region's love for spiced coconut-based dishes.

  • Pinangat

    - Pinangat, is a well-loved Bicolano dish made of layered taro leaves stuffed with spiced meat or shrimp, wrapped together, and slow-cooked in coconut milk with chili. It is rich, creamy, and mildly spicy—showcasing Bicol's signature use of gata (coconut milk) and sili (chili) in cooking.

  • Sinantolan

    - Sinantolan is a Bicolano dish made from grated santol (cotton fruit) rind cooked in coconut milk with garlic, onions, chili, and sometimes meat or shrimp. It has a unique blend of sour, creamy, and spicy flavors, reflecting Bicol's love for gata and spicy dishes.

  • Pancit Bato

    - Pancit Bato is a unique Bicolano noodle dish named after the town of Bato in Camarines Sur, where its distinct noodles are made. It is typically stir-fried with vegetables, pork, or shrimp, and has a smoky, chewy texture. A local favorite, it’s often served during gatherings and fiestas.
